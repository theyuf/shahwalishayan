DESIGN

My design services include a lot of different sub-categories to help non-profits and social enterprises increase their digital footprint. All forms of digital media and marketing are very visual forms and organisations need to make sure that all the content that they put out is in coherence with their brand strategy and mission. 

A lot of non-profits and social enterprises struggle in terms of producing aesthetically pleasing designs which could range anywhere from website design to the social media posts.
Here is a list of common mistakes that non-profits make when it comes to design:

  • USING TOO MUCH CONTENT - when it comes to getting the message across, a lot of non-profits and social enterprises use content-heavy designs on their websites and other forms of marketing. This is not only unappealing to the eyes but also exhausts the user as soon as they come in contact with your content.
  • OVERWHELMING THE READER - Typically, a user will want to make an online donation, sign up to volunteer or learn about your programs, there is a high risk of overwhelming the reader and losing their attention when you bombard them with a lot of text.
  • NOT SHARING VISUAL CONTENT - 'A picture is worth a thousand words' - the saying goes well with this point. Many non-profits and social enterprises show too much text content and do not use enough visuals to engage with potential customers, which is when they lose their interest.
  • NOT BEING MOBILE FRIENDLY - websites and all social media marketing posts need to be mobile friendly. More than 80% of your potential audience uses their mobile phones to engage with the content being put out.
